Planned Curriculum

What's Coming

Practical, hands-on training built around real simulation workflows — not just theory.

Module 1

CFD Fundamentals

Governing equations, discretization schemes, boundary conditions, convergence criteria, and turbulence model selection for practical simulations.

Module 2

Meshing & Geometry

Structured vs unstructured meshing, y+ requirements, mesh quality metrics, and best practices for complex geometries including engine cylinders.

Module 3

CONVERGE CFD for Combustion

Case setup, autonomous meshing, combustion chemistry integration, and post-processing reactive flow simulations in CONVERGE CFD.

Module 4

Turbulence Modeling

k-ε, k-ω SST, LES and DNS — when to use each, calibration strategies, and validation against experimental data.

Module 5

Spray & Multiphase

Lagrangian particle tracking, droplet breakup models, evaporation, and spray-combustion coupling for direct-injection engines.

Module 6

Industry Case Studies

End-to-end simulation walkthroughs: IC engine in-cylinder flow, gas turbine combustor, and aftertreatment systems with real datasets.

// A Taste of Module 4
The Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes momentum equation — the foundation of every k-ε and k-ω SST turbulence model you'll configure in CONVERGE CFD. Module 4 covers where the Reynolds stress term -ρ·u'u' comes from, why it needs closure, and how to pick the right model for your flow.
